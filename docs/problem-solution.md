# Problem & Solution

## Problem

Cyber fraud often begins with convincing social engineering:

- Fake KYC or bank account blocking alerts
- OTP and UPI PIN requests
- Suspicious shortened links
- QR code refund traps
- Fake job offers with registration fees
- Instant loan scams with processing fees
- Lottery, reward, or gift claims
- Police, courier, bank, or government impersonation

Citizens may not know which warning signs matter. Police teams may receive reports in unstructured formats and need a quick way to prioritize high-risk cases.

## Solution

CyberShield Police provides a local web app that:

- Accepts suspicious message text and URLs
- Produces a risk score and risk level
- Detects a likely scam category
- Shows triggered risk factors
- Explains the concern in citizen-friendly language
- Generates a police-oriented summary
- Stores every report as a case
- Shows analytics for police monitoring
- Exports a case report as PDF

## Why This Prototype Is Practical

- It does not require paid APIs.
- It runs locally on a laptop.
- It uses transparent weighted rules that judges can inspect.
- It includes synthetic demo data for safe presentation.
- It is scoped for one active developer while still showing end-to-end value.

## Expected Impact

For citizens:

- Faster recognition of risky messages
- Clear next steps before money or credentials are lost
- More confidence when reporting suspicious activity

For police:

- Structured reports instead of scattered text
- Prioritized high-risk and critical cases
- Category and platform analytics
- Exportable summaries for follow-up

## Ethical Position

CyberShield Police is a triage tool, not a final authority. Risk scoring supports human judgment and should be combined with official investigation procedures.
